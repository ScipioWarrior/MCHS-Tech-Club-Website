What are the harms of E-Waste?


E-Waste is a problem because many of these objects include dangerous chemicals that can leak into the soil when not properly disposed of. Do you remember the last time you disposed of batteries? Did you just throw them out? When batteries and other forms of e-waste end up in landfills, they can contaminate the soil with toxins like:

  • Mercury
  • Beryllium
  • Chromium
  • Lead
  • Barium
  • Cobalt
  • Arsenic

E-Waste is also shipped overseas to developing nations to be incinerated, often to extract metals and ores, such as copper from wiring. This process causes very harmful air pollution, leading to widespread respiratory illness.
