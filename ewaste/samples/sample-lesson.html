Lesson 1: What's HTML?


HTML a "markup language" used universally throughout the web. When you visit a website, it tells your web browser what to display, and how to display it; it's kind of like a set of instructions that the creator of the website writes out. These instructions are written in the language of HTML "markup", or code. As a brand new web developer, you'll be the one telling the web browser what to do!


HTML has been around since the beginnings of the Internet. Back in 1989, after about 9 years of consideration, a physicist by the name of Tim Berners-Lee proposed a hypertext system for the web. You don't need to be a physicist to understand HTML though; the final HyperText Markup Language (HTML!) standard adopted in 1995 is one of the easiest and most versatile type of coding you can learn!


HTML is occasionally revised to meet the demands of the evolving web. One of the most notable and recent changes was the introduction of HTML5 - the newest standard and the one you will be taught going forward. Think about what you do on the web now - watch YouTube videos? Listen to Pandora? Post pictures to Instagram? These types of multimedia over the Internet were not even dreamt of back in 1989, but are now commonplace. HTML5 was created to simplify the process of putting that type of content onto your website. 


			<!DOCTYPE html>
			<html>

			<p>Hello world</p>
			</body>
			</html>
